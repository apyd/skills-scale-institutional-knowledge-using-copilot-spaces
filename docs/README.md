# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management docs. This folder contains the process standards, guidelines, and reference materials that guide how OctoAcme plans, executes, and ships work. Whether you are onboarding as a new team member or looking to refresh your understanding of a specific phase, start here.

## Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured lifecycle approach with five key phases: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution**, **Release**, and **Closure & Retrospective**. Each phase is guided by clear principles that emphasize customer value, iterative delivery, clear ownership, data-informed decisions, and psychological safety. During Initiation, teams validate business needs and create a lightweight Project One-pager to align stakeholders before committing resources. Planning transforms the approved initiative into an actionable backlog by breaking work into shippable increments with defined acceptance criteria and effort estimates.

Execution & Tracking uses a structured team rhythm — daily standups, weekly syncs, and regular demos — to surface blockers early and keep work on track. Quality is ensured through a combination of unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA sign-off before any release. Release & Deployment is standardized with documented pre-release checklists and rollback plans so that every production deployment is safe and observable. Retrospectives after each sprint or milestone capture learnings and feed a continuous improvement cycle that evolves the team's practices over time.

Central to OctoAcme's success are clearly defined roles — Project Manager, Product Manager, Developers, and QA — and a consistent, multi-layered communication strategy that includes weekly syncs, daily standups, stakeholder update emails, and ad-hoc escalation paths. Together, these practices ensure that every project is delivered with transparency, accountability, and a focus on long-term quality.
