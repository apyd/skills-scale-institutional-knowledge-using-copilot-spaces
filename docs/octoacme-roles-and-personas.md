# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
Responsible for ensuring quality standards. Oversees QA planning, test case creation, and sign-off at release.

### Responsibilities
- Collaborate with Developers and Project Managers to review acceptance criteria
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Define and maintain QA processes and test plans
- Track and report defects through resolution
- Provide final quality sign-off before release

### Goals
- Maintain a high quality bar across all deliverables
- Achieve zero critical escapes to production

### Typical Communication
- Sign-off comments in pull requests
- QA status updates in sprint syncs and release reviews

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Bridges business needs with technical solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document requirements from stakeholders
- Clarify stakeholder priorities and success criteria
- Help the Product Manager refine and detail user stories
- Validate that solutions meet business goals during UAT

### Goals
- Produce clear, actionable requirements
- Ensure alignment between business objectives and technical implementation

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Backlog refinement sessions with the product and engineering team

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
Focuses on user experience and interface design.

### Responsibilities
- Translate requirements into user flows, wireframes, and polished UI designs
- Conduct usability reviews and gather user feedback during design sprints
- Maintain and evolve the product's design system
- Collaborate with Developers to ensure accurate implementation

### Goals
- Deliver high usability across all product surfaces
- Maintain a consistent and accessible design language

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with product and engineering stakeholders
- Hand-off sessions with developers to clarify design intent

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Documents technical and user-facing workflows.

### Responsibilities
- Create release notes, onboarding guides, and product documentation
- Collaborate with all roles to ensure documentation is timely and accurate
- Ensure documentation is accessible and easy to understand
- Maintain and update existing documentation as the product evolves

### Goals
- Enable self-serve documentation for users and team members
- Reduce support load through clear and comprehensive docs

### Typical Communication
- Feature reviews to capture documentation requirements
- Documentation syncs with product, engineering, and support teams

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Manages CI/CD pipelines, environments, and release automation.

### Responsibilities
- Support build, test, and deploy stages for developers and QA
- Ensure reliability and performance of infrastructure
- Advise on operational monitoring and incident response strategies
- Automate environment provisioning and configuration management

### Goals
- Achieve high deployment frequency with low change failure rate
- Maintain stable, observable, and reproducible environments

### Typical Communication
- Incident response coordination and post-mortems
- Infrastructure planning syncs with engineering and operations

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Advocate for secure development practices within the project team.

### Responsibilities
- Review features and designs for potential security risks
- Promote secure coding standards and best practices
- Facilitate security awareness training for the team
- Collaborate with DevOps and Developers to triage and mitigate vulnerabilities

### Goals
- Ensure the product is secure by design
- Maintain compliance with relevant security standards

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during feature design and development
- Threat modeling sessions with engineering and architecture leads
